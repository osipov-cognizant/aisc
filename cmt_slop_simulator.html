How the synthetic data maps to the survey workbook

Each dataset is generated to match the columns of the corresponding sheet in the workbook. IDs are stable across datasets within the same export build — the AssociateIDs in 01_Roster · Section A are referenced by the same IDs in 02_Form1_AuthorPost and 06_Form5_IntCoding; team IDs match across team-level forms; PlanIDs in 01_Roster · Section E match those in 05_Form4_Panelist. Build with Mixed RCT as the source to get a coherent set of related rows across all sheets.

The replicate-level summary dataset is different in shape — one row per replicate of the Mixed scenario, with columns for replicate index, Cohen's d, raw mean gap, p-value, decision branch, and significance flag. This is useful for power-curve and sensitivity analyses inside Excel.

How to read the diagram

Total count flowing through each column should be conserved across stages — the only stage where counts decrease is the peer-review filter (proposals failing peer review drop out of the pool). The relative widths of the ribbons entering the grade-band column are the operational signal: under Osipov's central claim, the LLM ribbon entering the High-grade band should be visibly thinner than the Workshop ribbon entering the same band, even though both source flows started at roughly the same width.

Counts shown are scaled to a representative single-cycle cohort using the simulation's per-arm sample sizes and the mixed-scenario coin-flip probability. To see how counts flow under different parameter choices, adjust the per-arm distribution means or the P(LLM) probability and click Rebuild from latest simulation.

Simulation methods

Each replicate of a scenario generates one synthetic onboarding-cycle dataset under the configured parameters. Panel grades for each plan are drawn from a truncated normal on [1.0, 5.0] with the configured arm mean and SD, plus additive zero-mean noise terms for team capability, panelist rater, and cycle. Each plan receives 3 panelist grades; the panelist-averaged grade is the unit of analysis.

For each replicate the simulator computes the mean grade by arm, Welch's two-sample t-statistic and its degrees of freedom, the two-sided p-value from a t-CDF approximation, and Cohen's d using the pooled SD. Across replicates the simulator reports the mean and 95% CI on d (percentile method on the replicate distribution), the empirical power (share of replicates with p < α), and the empirical Type-S error rate (share of significant replicates with the wrong sign).

Random number generation

A seeded mulberry32 PRNG drives all draws; the seed is shown on the dashboard and can be changed for reproducibility checks. Normal variates use Box-Muller. Truncation on [1, 5] is implemented by rejection.

Decision branches (per protocol §10)

  • Branch 1 — significant effect with d ≥ 0.4: policy change + cross-vertical replication.
  • Branch 2 — significant with 0.15 ≤ d < 0.4: targeted intervention.
  • Branch 3 — null (non-significant) or d < 0.15: report null finding, examine alternative formulations.

Default parameter rationale

Default per-arm means (LLM 3.10, workshop 3.55) yield a population-level d ≈ 0.55, in the middle of the protocol's pre-registered prediction range (d 0.4–0.8). Author interview behavior defaults follow the predictions in the protocol's mediation hypothesis (H3): slop authors show ~14s mean latency vs ~6s for workshop authors, and ~35% direct-answer rate vs ~72%. Defaults are illustrative — adjust to test the study's robustness to alternative truth states.

What the simulator does NOT do

The simulator generates synthetic outcome data only. It does not model interactions with team-side LLM use during the implementation phase (H5 exploratory) — this would require additional structural assumptions and is best examined after live data are collected. It also does not model selective compliance with the upstream coin flip; this can be tested by running Scenario C with an unbalanced P(LLM) value.
